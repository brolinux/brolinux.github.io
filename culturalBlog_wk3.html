Visual Thinkers

After taking the test I came out as a visual thinker, though I think that as a heavy radio listener I also learn through listening and writing things down, as well as drawing them out on the page.


Some habits of visual thikers are:

  • Organizing the information into groups by the order they unfold on the page.
  • Drawing the information to better see the link between tasks and events.
  • Wording mini essays to ourselves of what events mean and how they unfold.
  • Generally speaking...What aids in seeing processes big and bold and simplified.


Visual thinkers need to feel they can visualize the problem before coming up with a solution. The graphical interface we create on paper becomes a map to the solution.

I definitely catch myself scribbling something down next to a note I took, just to ensure it's really in there. I'm always grateful an article had a chart or a graph to support its basis. I like pictures and visual guides next to long blocks of text. All this is helpful, but not absolutely necessary.
