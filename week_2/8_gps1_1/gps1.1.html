Overview:


Pair Programming is essential at DBC's Phase 0 for all students. It solidifies the concept group collaboration, which is at the core of any professional development experience.

  • Staying on the Same Page
  • Promote Listening
  • Promote Independent Thinking
  • Mirrors Professional Development Environments

Staying on the Same Page
When more than one developer is working on a project, it is easy for them to interpret the requirements differently or create conflicting implementations. When they are pair programming, they are constantly collaborating on what and how to code so they come away with the same understanding of what needs to be done, especially when working separately later on.

Promote Listening
It is easy for one developer's ideas to dominate when coding a project. By programming in pairs and allowing one developer to provide direction while the other codes,that ensures that both developers' thoughts on the project are considered.

Promote Independent Thinking
Without the discrete separation of driver and navigator roles, it's possible for developers to fall into a follower role where they defer their opinion to other developers. Splitting the responsibilities between drivers and navigators and the dialog it creates prevents developers from passing over opportunities of providing their input.

Mirrors Professional Development Environments
Large projects require multiple developers and multiple developers need coordination. In these cases, it is necessary work together on programming for all the other reasons discussed. Therefore this method of development is used in the professional environment to ensure quality work among more than one developer when several large parts of the project are in motion.
